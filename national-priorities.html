Presidential Decree

Azerbaijan 2030: National Priorities for Socio-Economic Development

February 2, 2021 • Republic of Azerbaijan

On February 2, 2021, the President of the Republic of Azerbaijan approved "Azerbaijan 2030: National Priorities for Socio-Economic Development". This strategic document outlines the long-term development vector of the country for the next decade, with a strong emphasis on sustainability and green growth.

"A clean environment and a 'green growth' country is one of the five national priorities for the next decade."

Strategic Priorities

The document defines five National Priorities to be achieved by 2030:

  1. A steadily growing competitive economy;
  2. A society based on dynamic, inclusive and social justice;
  3. Competitive human capital and space for modern innovations;
  4. Great Return to the liberated territories;
  5. Clean environment and "green growth" country.

Focus on Green Energy

The fifth priority, "Clean environment and 'green growth' country," specifically targets environmental rehabilitation and the rapid expansion of green energy. Key objectives include:

  • Increasing the share of renewable energy sources in the installed capacity of electricity generation to 30% by 2030.
  • Reducing greenhouse gas emissions and aligning with the Paris Agreement goals.
  • Transforming the liberated territories (Karabakh and East Zangezur) into a "Green Energy Zone".
  • Improving energy efficiency across all sectors of the economy.

This priority sets the stage for massive investments in solar, wind, and hydro power, and mandates the modernization of the regulatory framework to support this transition.
