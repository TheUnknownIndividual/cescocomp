üìñ Executive Summary

Presidential Order ‚Ññ1209, issued on May 29, 2019, marks a pivotal moment in Azerbaijan's energy transformation. This comprehensive decree establishes the legal and institutional framework for accelerating reforms in the energy sector, with a particular emphasis on renewable energy development, market liberalization, and regulatory modernization.

Strategic Importance:

This order serves as the foundational legal instrument that enabled the subsequent passage of the 2021 Renewable Energy Sources Law and established the framework for Azerbaijan's ambitious '30 by 2030' renewable energy targets.

The decree mandates comprehensive reforms across electricity production, gas supply, regulatory frameworks, and investment conditions, positioning Azerbaijan as a regional leader in the energy transition.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Legislative Mandates

  • Renewable Energy Framework Law
    The Ministry of Energy was tasked with developing the "Law on the Use of Renewable Energy Sources in Electricity Production" in coordination with the Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Economy, SOCAR, Azerenergy JSC, and Azeri≈üƒ±q JSC. This law was successfully enacted in May 2021.
  • Regulatory Authority Establishment
    Creation of legislation for energy and utilities regulatory bodies, establishing independent oversight mechanisms for market operations, tariff setting, and compliance monitoring.
  • Energy Sector Modernization Laws
    New drafts of the "Law on Energy" and "Law on Gas Supply" were commissioned to replace outdated frameworks and align with international best practices and EU energy directives.
  • International Consultant Engagement
    Authorization for the Ministry of Energy, in coordination with the Ministry of Finance, to engage internationally recognized consulting firms to support implementation and ensure world-class technical standards.

üéØ Implementation & Action Plan

Following Presidential Order ‚Ññ1209, the Ministry of Energy issued Order F-52 on June 7, 2019, establishing a detailed implementation roadmap. This comprehensive action plan created specialized working groups and assigned clear responsibilities across government agencies.

üöÄ Achieved Outcomes & Impact

  • Legislative Success: The 2021 Renewable Energy Sources Law (Law ‚Ññ339-VIQ) was successfully enacted, establishing comprehensive legal frameworks for RES development, grid connection rights, and investment incentives.
  • Market Liberalization: Introduction of competitive auction mechanisms, long-term Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs), and transparent tariff structures that attracted over $2 billion in renewable energy investments.
  • Institutional Development: Establishment of the Energy Regulatory Agency (AREA) with independent authority over market operations, licensing, and dispute resolution.
  • International Partnerships: Engagement with leading firms including EBRD, IFC, ADB, and specialized energy consultants from EU member states, bringing world-class expertise to Azerbaijan's reforms.
  • Investment Climate: Creation of favorable conditions including 30-year tax holidays, VAT exemptions, and streamlined permitting processes, resulting in major contracts with Masdar (10 GW), ACWA Power (1 GW), and bp.
