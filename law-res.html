Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan

On the Use of Renewable Energy Sources in Electricity Production

Law №339-VIQ • May 31, 2021 • Republic of Azerbaijan

This Law determines the legal, economic, and organizational bases for the use of renewable energy sources (RES) in the production of electricity, establishes support mechanisms to promote electricity production from renewable energy sources, and regulates relations arising in this field, in accordance with paragraph 11 of Part I of Article 94 of the Constitution of the Republic of Azerbaijan.

"A foundational framework defining renewable energy sources, support mechanisms like guaranteed tariffs, and the rights and duties of producers and grid operators in Azerbaijan."

Chapter 1. General Provisions

Article 1. Basic Definitions. The law defines key terms such as:

  • Renewable Energy Sources (RES): Sources constantly existing or regularly appearing in the environment (hydro, wind, solar, geothermal, biomass, wave, tidal, river flow energy, etc.).
  • Guaranteed Tariff: A tariff determined for producers selected through auctions or direct attraction in RES areas as a support mechanism for each kilowatt-hour of electricity supplied to the grid.
  • Active Consumer: A legal or natural person who, in addition to consuming electricity, produces up to 150 kW (inclusive) of electricity from renewable energy sources.
  • RES Territory: Land plots or water objects determined by the relevant executive authority as having RES potential and being suitable for electricity production.

Article 3. Principles of State Regulation. State regulation is based on principles including the application of support mechanisms, equal access to these mechanisms, transparency, equal grid access for producers, application of international standards, and efficient use of RES potential.

Article 4. Duties of the State. The state is responsible for formulating policy, adopting action plans and normative acts, creating a favorable investment climate, identifying RES territories, applying support mechanisms, and training personnel.

Chapter 2. Support for RES Usage

Article 5. Support Mechanisms. The following mechanisms are applied:

  • Support for electricity production in RES territories through guaranteed tariffs and other means.
  • Support for active consumers (prosumers).
  • Support for scientific research.
  • Tax and customs incentives under the Tax Code and Law on Customs Tariff.

Article 6. RES Territories. An "Atlas of Renewable Energy Sources Potential" is created to identify suitable lands. Proposals for designating lands as RES territories are evaluated based on potential, grid connection possibilities, and property rights.

Article 8. Selection of Producers. Producers for RES territories are selected via auction or direct attraction. Auctions select the bidder offering the lowest electricity price. Direct attraction is used for strategic pilot projects or if auctions fail.

Article 9. Tariffs. Except for guaranteed tariffs, electricity is sold at wholesale prices. Guaranteed tariffs are determined through auctions or agreements in direct attraction cases and apply for the duration determined in the purchase agreement.

Article 10. Active Consumer Support. Consumers can install stations up to 150 kW. A "net metering" style mechanism allows them to supply excess energy to the grid and offset it against consumption. If net generation is positive, the supplier pays the consumer at the wholesale tariff; if negative, the consumer pays the retail tariff.

Chapter 3. Technical and Organizational Requirements

Article 12-14. Construction follows the Urban Planning and Construction Code. Rights to land are acquired per the Land Code. Generation capacities above certain limits require permission under the "Law on Licenses and Permits".

Article 15. Information System. An information system is created to track RES potential, generation statistics, saved natural gas, and carbon quota revenues.

Article 16. Certificates of Origin. Certificates confirming the source of RES electricity are issued to producers to facilitate tracking and support mechanism usage.

Article 17. Grid Connection. RES producers have priority in transmission and distribution. Grid connection contracts are signed with system operators, and grid development plans must account for new RES stations.

Chapter 4. Rights and Duties

Article 18. Producers. Producers have the right to use support mechanisms, sell electricity, and access the grid. They must use advanced technologies and pay connection costs. Producers (except hydro >10MW) are exempt from balancing responsibility unless they voluntarily participate.

Article 19. System Operators. Operators (transmission/distribution) must connect stations to the optimal point, transmit/distribute RES electricity, and modernize the grid to accommodate connections.

Qanun № 339-VIQ: "Elektrik enerjisi istehsalında bərpa olunan enerji mənbələrindən istifadə haqqında"

Bu Qanun bərpa olunan enerji mənbələrindən istifadənin hüquqi və iqtisadi əsaslarını müəyyən edir. Qanun zəmanətli tariflər, hərraclar vasitəsilə istehsalçıların seçilməsi, "aktiv istehlakçı" (net-metering) mexanizmi və bərpa olunan enerji mənbələrinin ərazilərinin müəyyənləşdirilməsi kimi vacib məsələləri tənzimləyir. Məqsəd ölkənin enerji təhlükəsizliyini gücləndirmək və təmiz enerji istehsalını təşviq etməkdir.
