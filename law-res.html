Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan

On the Use of Renewable Energy Sources in the Production of Electricity

Law ‚Ññ 339-VIQ ‚Ä¢ May 31, 2021 ‚Ä¢ Republic of Azerbaijan

This Law defines the legal, economic, and organizational bases for the use of renewable energy sources in the production of electricity in the Republic of Azerbaijan, aiming to create a favorable investment environment and ensure energy efficiency.

Chapter 1. General Provisions

Article 1. Basic Definitions

1.1. The following definitions are used in this Law:

  • 1.1.1. Renewable Energy Sources (RES): energy sources that are naturally replenished (solar, wind, hydro, biomass, geothermal, etc.);
  • 1.1.2. Active Consumer: A legal or natural person who produces electricity from renewable energy sources for their own consumption and has the right to transfer excess electricity to the grid;
  • 1.1.3. Producer: A qualified entity producing electricity from renewable energy sources;
  • 1.1.4. Guaranteed Tariff: A fixed tariff set for the purchase of electricity generated from renewable sources.

Article 2. Legislation on Renewable Energy Sources

The legislation of the Republic of Azerbaijan on the use of renewable energy sources consists of the Constitution of the Republic of Azerbaijan, international treaties to which the Republic of Azerbaijan is a party, the Law "On Energy", this Law, and other normative legal acts.

Chapter 2. State Regulation and Support

State policy in the field of renewable energy aims to increase the share of RES in the energy balance, ensure energy security, and protect the environment. The state provides support mechanisms such as guaranteed tariffs, investment incentives, and simplified administrative procedures.

Article 6. Measurement and Metering

All electricity produced from renewable energy sources and supplied to the grid must be metered using modern counting equipment.

Chapter 3. Production and Grid Connection

Producers have the right to connect to the electricity grid in accordance with the Grid Code. The grid operator is obliged to prioritize the off-take of electricity generated from renewable sources, subject to grid stability requirements.

Article 10. Active Consumers

Active consumers can install RES plants (up to a limit established by the Cabinet of Ministers, typically 150 kW) and utilize the net metering mechanism. Excess electricity generated can be offset against consumption in future periods or sold to the energy supplier.
