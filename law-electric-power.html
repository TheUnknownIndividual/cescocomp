    Law of the Republic of Azerbaijan

    Law on Electric Power

    Law ‚Ññ 858-VIQ ‚Ä¢ April 11, 2023 ‚Ä¢ Signed by President Ilham Aliyev

    This Law determines the legal, economic, and organizational bases for the production, transmission, distribution, import, export, and supply of electric power in the Republic of Azerbaijan. It regulates relations between electric power entities and consumers, aiming to ensure energy security, reliability, efficiency, and the development of a competitive electricity market.

    Chapter 1. General Provisions

    Article 1. Basic Concepts

    This article defines key terms such as "Active Consumer," "Bilateral Agreements," "Transmission System Operator," "Distribution System Operator," "Electricity Market Operator," and "Universal Supplier."

    Article 2. Legislation

    Legislation consists of the Constitution, international treaties, the Law on Energy, this Law, and other normative legal acts.

    Chapter 2. Organization and Management

    Article 4. State Regulation

    The state regulates the sector to ensure sustainable development, security of supply, and protection of consumer rights. The Regulator plays a key role in licensing, tariff setting, and market monitoring.

    Chapter 3. Electricity Market

    Article 8. Market Structure

    The electricity market comprises wholesale and retail markets. The wholesale market includes bilateral contracts, Day-Ahead Market, and Balancing Market.

    Article 9. System Operations

    Transmission and Distribution System Operators are responsible for the safe and reliable operation of the grid, ensuring non-discriminatory access for all market participants.

    Chapter 4. Consumer Protection

    Article 12. Vulnerable Consumers

    Specific provisions protect vulnerable consumers, ensuring their access to electricity supply.

    Ilham Aliyev
    President of the Republic of Azerbaijan
